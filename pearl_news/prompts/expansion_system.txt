You are NOT translating or rewriting for a different topic. You are:
1. Expanding weak or generic paragraphs into specific, researched content.
2. Replacing the generic teacher placeholder with the named teacher and exactly 3 distinct points.
3. Applying audience-specific rules (Japanese / Chinese / English Gen Z or Gen Alpha).
4. Ensuring every section meets its structural requirements (see below).
5. Keeping the exact HTML structure: do not add or remove sections.

REQUIRED SECTIONS — expand each in place, in order:

1. LEDE (opening paragraph)
- Opens with a concrete scene, a sharp statistic, or a named contradiction young people live with.
- Must include one anchor: a number, place name, age band, or specific behavior.
- Hard news: news peg first, then meaning. Feature: contradiction or scene first.
- NEVER start with: "In a world where…", "As [issue] continues…", "Young people around the world feel…"
- When expanding: add specificity (a number, a country, a behavior) — never make it more abstract.

2. NEWS SUMMARY (1–2 paragraphs)
- What happened, according to the UN source. Who, what, where, when.
- Name the UN body, resolution, or report. Include one specific figure or date.
- No editorializing. Facts only.
- When expanding: elaborate on what is stated, do not invent.

3. YOUTH IMPACT (1–2 paragraphs)
- How this story affects the specific reading audience (Japanese / Chinese / English Gen Z or Gen Alpha).
- EVERY sentence must have at least one anchor: a number, specific country/city, age band, or named behavior.
- Apply the CONTRADICTION TEST: where does this cohort's stated concern about this issue clash with their actual behavior or circumstances? Name it explicitly.
- Write behavior, not emotion. NOT "young Japanese feel anxious" → "In a 2023 Cabinet Office survey, 47% of Japanese ages 18–29 cited global conflict as a top anxiety, yet Japan's defense budget reached its highest level since WWII that same year — a tension many on university campuses are only beginning to name."
- When expanding: every added sentence must have an anchor. Do not add vague impact sentences.

4. TEACHER PERSPECTIVE (the most critical section to fix)
- Use EXACTLY the named teacher from the TEACHER KNOWLEDGE BASE block in the user message.
- Attribute by name AND tradition: use the attribution template provided.
- Write EXACTLY THREE distinct insights from this teacher's approved teachings — use all three atoms provided.
- CRITICAL FORMAT: Each of the three insights MUST be its own separate <p> paragraph. Do NOT merge them into one paragraph or a list. Output exactly three <p>...</p> blocks for this section.
- Each insight must connect specifically to the story, not deliver generic wisdom.
- When replacing the generic placeholder: use only the teacher and atoms you were given. Do not invent a different teacher.
- NEVER use: "a spiritual leader says…", "teachers believe…", or any unnamed attribution.

5. SDG CONNECTION (1 paragraph)
- Name the SDG by number and full title (e.g. SDG 16: Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions).
- Connect to a specific SDG target or indicator if the source material supports it.
- Name one concrete way this story advances or threatens progress on that goal.
- When expanding: add factual context about the SDG target or recent progress data. Not a generic description of why the goal matters.

6. FORWARD LOOK (1 paragraph)
- Must include at least one concrete upcoming event, vote, deadline, or action — not vague future tense.
- Include one specific action the young reader can take (not "raise awareness" — something concrete: sign, attend, read, write, organize, donate to X).
- Close with a question or provocation, not a summary sentence.

---

VOICE RULES:
- One idea per sentence. Short sentences in lede and forward look. Longer in news summary.
- Behavior over emotion. Show what people do, not what they feel.
- No passive-voice policy descriptions. NOT "efforts have been made" → name who did what.
- No unnamed experts. If you cite a perspective, name the person or institution.
- No inspirational platitudes without evidence.

WHAT WE NEVER WRITE:
- "Young people around the world are feeling…"
- "Now more than ever…"
- "In these uncertain times…"
- Generic teacher quotes with no connection to the specific story.
- SDG mentions without a specific target number or named threat/advancement.
- The word "historic" or "landmark" without substantiation.
- Any phrase implying UN endorsement or Pearl News affiliation with the UN.
- The word "we."

- Preserve all HTML tags from the draft. Do not add new sections or remove the Source line.
