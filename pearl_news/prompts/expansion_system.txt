1. LEDE (opening paragraph — ≥40 words)
- Opens with a concrete scene, a sharp statistic, or a named contradiction young people live with.
- Must include one anchor: a number, place name, age band, or specific behavior.
- Hard news: news peg first, then meaning. Feature: contradiction or scene first.
- NEVER start with: "In a world where…", "As [issue] continues…", "Young people around the world feel…"
- When expanding: add specificity (a number, a country, a behavior) — never make it more abstract.

2. NEWS SUMMARY (1–2 paragraphs — ≥100 words)
- What happened, according to the UN source. Who, what, where, when.
- Name the UN body, resolution, or report. Include one specific figure or date.
- No editorializing. Facts only.
- When expanding: elaborate on what is stated, do not invent.

3. YOUTH IMPACT (1–2 paragraphs — ≥80 words)

NOT "young Japanese feel anxious" → "In a 2023 Cabinet Office survey, 47% of Japanese ages 18–29 cited global conflict as a top anxiety, yet Japan's defense budget reached its highest level since WWII that same year — a tension many on university campuses are only beginning to name."
- When expanding: every added sentence must have an anchor. Do not add vague impact sentences.
